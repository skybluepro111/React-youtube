Learn to train on your own #interpretameWebinar

Good morning,

We are excited to personally send you this invitation to our webinar Cómo entreno mis habilidades con Interprétame in which we will present different preparatory activities the platform allows.

Mental agility, bidirectionality, improving a foreign language ... Learn to make your own Interpreting exercises and to sharpen the skills all good interpreters need.

We will ask for volunteers to accept challenges live. If Interpreting is your field, seize this opportunity to gain autonomy in your training. Register now and share the information with your colleagues. The webinar will be in Spanish. If this is not one of the languages you can understand, do not worry, let us know you are interested in attending and we will prepare another one in English within the next months.

It's not only been the preparation of this webinar that has been keeping us busy, but also other exciting plans we are setting in motion. For these, we are working on a two-minute pitch video depicting the three target groups that can benefit from interpretame.com to run a crowdfunding campaign.

If you are interested in giving a hand to this couple of entrepreneurs trying to bring a great project to the next level, contact us. Me and my partner would add your name and face in a section dedicated to thanking the people who are contributing to make this project possible and would offer you Premium access to the platform when it's ready.

Last, another brave interpreter dared to write in our Revista digital. Have you ever considered what role does seduction play in relationships with customers? Do you think all interpreters use note taking? Javier, from @Tradosfera, tells us firsthand how he interacts with his customers as a technical and international business interpreter in his article Mi experiencia como intérprete autónomo. The article has already received more than 300 visits in the past weeks; If you have not been one of them, we recommend you to spend two minutes and share your impressions in the comments below.

That's all for now. Have some great and productive Easter holidays!
